Yes. That’s the right pause to take.

Before we add fields, we should decide what data is actually worth collecting, because better vector quality comes from better signals, not just more columns.

For a dating app like this, I’d think in 4 buckets:

1. Music Identity
This is what they love.
Good fields:

selected_moods
era_preference
energy_preference
discovery_style
Example: mainstream, underground, mixed
listening_contexts
Example: gym, night drive, studying, party, heartbreak
top_tracks_text or favorite_lyrics_text
Free-text can be surprisingly rich
music_quiz_answers
2. Personal Identity
This is who they are, not just what they play.
Good fields:

bio
personality_prompts
Example: “ideal weekend”, “what I value”, “green flags”
relationship_intent
Dating, casual, long-term, friends
lifestyle_tags
Introvert, extrovert, ambitious, spiritual, creative
humor_style
communication_style
3. Attraction / Preference Data
This is critical and should be separate from identity.
Good fields:

preferred_vibe_tags
Calm, confident, funny, deep, adventurous
preferred_music_traits
Someone with similar taste, opposite taste, concert person, etc.
dealbreakers
age_range_preference
distance_preference
later: learned preference from swipe behavior
4. Behavioral Signals
Usually not collected directly at signup; learned over time.
Good fields:

like rate
pass rate
response rate
time to swipe
active hours
consistency of engagement
who they like versus who likes them back
What most dating apps effectively use:

profile text
prompts/short answers
declared preferences
interaction behavior
photo embeddings / attractiveness patterns
location and activity recency
message/reply behavior
implicit preference learning from swipes
What I would not do:

Don’t dump everything into music_vector
Don’t ask 30 onboarding questions on day 1
Don’t collect weak/noisy fields just because they sound advanced
What I would do for VibeMatch first:
Collect only high-signal fields that fit your concept.

Best first music-enrichment set:

selected_moods
era_preference
listening_contexts
discovery_style
music_quiz_answers
Best first non-music fields to plan for:

relationship_intent
personality_prompts
preferred_vibe_tags
distance_preference
So the architecture I’d aim for is:

music_vector
Artists + tags + moods + era + contexts + music quiz
personality_vector
Bio + prompts + lifestyle/personality fields
preference_vector
Preferred vibe tags + later swipe-learning
behavior_vector
Generated from usage, not manually entered
That’s probably the cleanest and strongest version.
